The Internet

The Internet is the physical structure that connects devices together. It predates the World Wide Web by over two decades and was developed through the work of dozens of pioneering scientists, programmers, and engineers.

The World Wide Web

The World Wide Web was created by Sir Tim Berners-Lee, who wrote the foundational technologies:

  • HTTP: Hypertext Transfer Protocol
  • URL: Universal Resource Locator
  • HTML: Hypertext Markup Language

He published the first website on 20 December 1990 and later founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), which developed technologies like CSS (Cascading Style Sheets) that shape modern web design.

Shaping Modern Life

Sir Tim Berners-Lee made his idea freely available, with no patents or royalties, enabling the Web to become “the fastest growing communications medium of all time.” It has revolutionised global connectivity, allowing instant communication across the world.

About Tim Berners-Lee

Sir Tim Berners-Lee is the inventor of the World Wide Web. He shaped the way we interact with the internet today by designing its key technologies.

Tim Brayshaw BEng VR


My name is Tim Brayshaw and I am a teacher with a diverse background in education and the military. My career began with a focus on developing computing curriculums for primary schools when most classrooms had just a couple of computers. I facilitated the creation of computer suites, allowing entire classes to participate in lessons rather than just one or two students at a time.

I also served as a reservist in the British Army with the Royal Signals. My responsibilities included training new soldiers in their trades, which was immensely rewarding and inspired me to pursue a career in teaching.

Currently, I work as a teacher in post-16 education and live in Essex, England. My journey has been driven by a passion for teaching and a commitment to helping students develop the skills they need for the future.

Employment

Sixth Form College Colchester

As a Computer Science Teacher at Colchester Sixth Form College, I teach Information Technology and Computer Science at BTEC Level 3 and A-Level. My responsibilities include developing and updating course materials, delivering engaging lessons and providing academic support and mentorship.

British Army Volunteer Reserve

As a British Army Reservist in the Royal Signals for 18 years, I was responsible for teaching and training personnel. I led a detachment that ensured effective communication between units and played a crucial role in maintaining operational efficiency. My duties included developing training programs and overseeing the implementation of communication systems while providing leadership and support to my team.

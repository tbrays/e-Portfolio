Post on using e-portfolio

Currently I am a teacher employed in post 16 education. It is part of my responsibility to work with students, finding paths to their futures, whether this is moving on to university or finding employment opportunities. Beetham (2010) describes an e-portfolio as “a collection of digital resources that can be used for review, reflection and personal development planning” An e-portfolio can have different emphasis, for example, development, showcase or assessment. During the two years of study, the students make use of ‘Morrisby’ to keep a running journal of their experiences and achievements. This can then be used to create a showcase e-portfolio for use in applications.

E-portfolios provide unbridled access and distribution, allowing them to be updated and shared from anywhere to anywhere. This provides the tools for reflective practice as suggested by Slepcevic & Stock (2018). It also allows for a variety of materials to be used, be it text, images, video or sound. This is beneficial to assisting learning and for bringing attention to potential employers.

A complication is that establishing an e-portfolio takes time and effort and can prove technically challenging dependent on the chosen format.

In summary, an e-portfolio has more benefit to student learning and career prospects, than any drawbacks. In my experience, e-portfolios have proven their use in further education and will provide the same benefits in higher education. For this reason, I will be happy to keep an e-portfolio during my studies and will enjoy developing it as a website.

Beetham, H. (2005) E-portfolios in post-16 learning in the UK: Developments, issues and opportunities. Slepcevic-Zach, P., & Stock, M. (2018) ePortfolio as a tool for reflection and self-reflection.
